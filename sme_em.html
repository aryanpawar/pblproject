SME : Systems In Mechanical Enginnering

Syllabus :(Units)

  1. Introduction of energy sources & its conversion
  2. Introduction to Thermal Engineering
  3. Vehicles and their Specifications
  4. Vehicle systems
  5. Introduction to Manufacturing
  6. Engineering Mechanisms and their application in Domestic Appliances

Resources & Books :

  • Nag, P. K., “Engineering Thermodynamics,'' Tata McGraw-Hill Publisher Co. Ltd
  • Chaudhari and Hajra, “Elements of Workshop Technology”, Volume I and II, Media Promoters and Publishers, Mumbai
  • Agrawal,Basant and Agrawal, C. M., (2008), “Basics of Mechanical Engineering”, John Wiley and Sons, USA
  • Rajput, R.K., (2007), “Basic Mechanical Engineering”, Laxmi Publications Pvt. Ltd.
  • Pravin Kumar, (2018), “ Basic Mechanical Engineering, 2nd Ed.”, Pearson (India) Ltd
  • Moran, M. J., Shapiro, H. N., Boettner, D. D., and Bailey, M. “Fundamentals of Engineering Thermodynamics”, Wiley
  • Surinder Kumar, (2011), “Basic of Mechanical Engineering”, Ane Books Pvt. Ltd. New Delhiy

Other Information :

For study of this subject you need to have some basic knowledge of thermodynamics , basic instruments that we use in our daily life , some formulae about work , power , energy , etc.

EM : Engineering Mechanics

Syllabus :(Units)

  1. Resolution and Composition of Forces
  2. Distributed Forces and Friction
  3. Equilibrium
  4. Analysis of Structures
  5. Kinematics of Particle
  6. Kinetics of Particle

Resources & Books :

  • Vector Mechanics for Engineers, by F. P. Beer and E. R. Johnson, McGraw-Hill Publication
  • Engineering Mechanics by R. C. Hibbeler, Pearson Education
  • Engineering Mechanics by S. P. Timoshenko and D. H. Young, McGraw- Hill publication
  • Engineering Mechanics by J. L. Meriam and Craige, John Willey
  • Engineering Mechanics by F L Singer, Harper and Rowe publication
  • Engineering Mechanics by A. P. Boresi and R. J. Schmidt, Brooks/Cole Publication

Other Information :

The subjects which further come in your engineering will be based on this subject like Strength of Materials, Mechanics Of Material, etc. This subjects gives you the basics for everything which are required further. So it is better to learn the concepts and everything in an sorted manner rather than studying just to pass the exam.
